# ROLL CHESS Hybrid Competition Rules v1.1

**Effective:** June 2026  
**Format:** Alternating mat rounds and chess games with linked consequences

---

## 1. Overview

ROLL CHESS is a hybrid competition where **mat performance directly modifies chess conditions**. Competitors alternate between:

1. **Mat Phase** — timed BJJ sparring or positional rounds
2. **Board Phase** — standard chess (rapid or blitz per event tier)

The system trains and tests whether physical pressure degrades tactical decision-making — and rewards competitors who manage both.

---

## 2. Equipment

- Standard chess set and clock (digital recommended)
- BJJ gi or no-gi per event rules
- Mat area and board area separated but visible to both competitors
- Optional: ROLL CHESS spectrum tracker (trainer app or official score sheet)

---

## 3. Match Structure

### 3.1 Default Format (Standard Hybrid)

| Segment | Duration | Notes |
|---------|----------|-------|
| Mat Round 1 | 5 min | Positional or rolling per division |
| Board Game 1 | 10+5 rapid | Consequences from Mat 1 apply |
| Mat Round 2 | 5 min | Fresh positional start |
| Board Game 2 | 10+5 rapid | Consequences from Mat 2 apply |

Best-of-two board games, or single combined score per event director discretion.

### 3.2 Sprint Format

One 4-minute mat round → one 5+3 blitz game. Used for brackets and open mat nights.

---

## 4. The Mat → Board Spectrum

Each competitor occupies a position on the **Mat → Board Spectrum** (0–100):

| Range | State | Board Effect |
|-------|-------|--------------|
| 0–29 | Mat-heavy | −30 sec starting time, no takeback offers |
| 30–44 | Fatigued | −15 sec starting time |
| 45–55 | Neutral | Standard time |
| 56–69 | Composed | +5 sec starting time |
| 70–100 | Board-ready | +10 sec, optional second takeback in casual tiers |

Spectrum updates after each mat round based on **Decision Quality Score** (DQS, §5).

---

## 5. Mat Phase Scoring (DQS)

Event staff or designated judge scores each mat round:

| Outcome | DQS | Spectrum Δ |
|---------|-----|------------|
| Dominant control / clean submission attempt | +15 to +20 | Toward Board |
| Solid positional maintenance | +5 to +10 | Slight toward Board |
| Neutral stalemate | 0 | No change |
| Repeated defensive errors | −5 to −10 | Toward Mat |
| Tap, stalling, or safety violation | −15 to −25 | Strong toward Mat |

Judges use observable criteria only — no subjective "style" points.

---

## 6. Board Phase Consequences

### 6.1 Time Adjustments

Apply spectrum modifier (§4) to **both** players independently before each board game starts.

### 6.2 Graduated Penalties (v1.1)

Penalties stack from mat phase — they are ** graduated**, not binary:

| Mat Event | Light Tier | Medium Tier | Heavy Tier |
|-----------|------------|-------------|------------|
| Single DQS −5 | Warning only | −5 sec | −10 sec |
| DQS −10 or worse | −5 sec | −15 sec | −30 sec |
| Tap in mat round | −10 sec | −20 sec | −45 sec + blitz only next board |
| Two consecutive bad rounds | −10 sec | −25 sec | Must play board before 60 sec recovery |

**Light Tier:** Training events, juniors, first-time competitors.  
**Medium Tier:** Default sanctioned events.  
**Heavy Tier:** Advanced / championship — full consequence stack.

Event director announces tier at registration.

### 6.3 Conduct Penalties

- Illegal mat techniques → immediate mat DQS −25 and board forfeit at TD discretion
- Chess rule violations (touch-move, illegal move) → standard USCF/FIDE penalty **plus** −5 spectrum toward Mat
- Unsportsmanlike conduct → remove spectrum bonuses for remainder of match

---

## 7. Winning the Match

### 7.1 Primary

Most board points (wins = 1, draw = 0.5 each) after scheduled games.

### 7.2 Tiebreakers (in order)

1. Aggregate spectrum position after final mat round (higher wins)
2. Combined mat DQS
3. Armageddon blitz board (neutral spectrum, no mat segment)

| Competitive | 5 min roll | 10+5 | Medium |

---

## 9. Official Roles

- **Mat Judge** — scores DQS, confirms spectrum shift
- **Board Arbiter** — chess rules, clock, time adjustments
- **Table Chief** — records spectrum, announces board clock settings

Minimum one official per active table.

---

## 10. Trainer App Alignment

The [ROLL CHESS Trainer v0.5](https://lylegill02-cpu.github.io/roll-chess/trainer.html) simulates these rules:

- **Training Mode** — no clock penalties (practice DQS and spectrum only)
- **Recovery Mode** — applies tiered time penalties after poor mat choices

Competition tiers map directly to trainer Recovery tiers for pre-event rehearsal.
